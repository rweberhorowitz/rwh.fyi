About Me

I’m an entry-level Public Relations professional with a strong background in organizing and leadership. Through my experiences with community volunteering and outreach, I’m a passionate and dedicated communicator with a love for helping others. I’m a confident public speaker and an innovative problem solver. Currently, I’m a student worker at NKU’s College of Informatics (COI). I design digital content for the COI’s social media and interview and engage with local organizations for the college. Additionally, I have had the privilege of planning and managing some of the COI’s biggest events! It brings me great joy to know that my efforts are making a difference in the lives of my fellow students. I also have a long history of community service and volunteer work; I’ve been an active member in my community for most of my life! Whether I was handing out meals and school supplies to families in need, assisting in coaching the local robotics team or volunteering my time to the Campbell County Band of Pride, I’ve always been on the lookout for ways to help others. When I’m not working or volunteering, you can often find me hanging out around campus. I love biking, reading, gardening and playing bass guitar. Most importantly, though, I’m a huge people-person; I’ll be friends with anyone!

Riley Weber-Horowitz

Northern Kentucky University Student
